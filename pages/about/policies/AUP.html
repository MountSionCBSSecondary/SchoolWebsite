School Internet Acceptable Usage Policy.

November 2018.


Rationale.

 

The aim of this acceptable usage policy (AUP) is to ensure that students get maximum benefit from the learning opportunities offered by the school’s ICT and internet resources in a safe and responsible manner. The school has two fully equipped computer rooms, one portable class set of twenty four chromebooks, all classrooms have a teacher’s computer with wired internet access and all classrooms and common areas have WIFI access.The school’s wifi system provides two networks, an open public network and a password protected private network. Internet use and access is considered a school resource and privilege. If the school’s AUP is not adhered to, this privilege will be withdrawn and appropriate sanctions will be imposed.

 

School Acceptable Use Strategy.

 

The school employs a number of strategies in order to maximise learning opportunities and reduce risks associated with the internet. These strategies include:

 

General

  • Open internet sessions will always be supervised by a teacher.

  • Filtering systems (HEANET) and or equivalent systems will be used where appropriate in order to minimise the risk of exposure to inappropriate material.

  • The school will regularly monitor students’ internet usage.

  • Each student will be issued with a username and password for their own network account. Students must use their own account at all times.

  • Students will be issued with a school email account for educational purposes only. This will be setup in such a way as to only allow sending and receiving messages within the mountsioncbssecondary.ie domain. This will facilitate communications between teachers and students.

  • Students and teachers will be provided with training in the area of internet usage and safety.

  • Uploading/downloading and installation of non approved software will not be permitted.

  • Virus protection software will be used and updated on a regular basis.

  • Students will treat each other with respect at all times and will not undertake any actions that may bring the school into disrepute.

  • Communication by edmodo/ google suite apps for education must be appropriate.


 

 

World Wide Web

  • Students will not visit internet sites that contain obscene, illegal, hateful or otherwise objectionable materials.

  • Students will use the internet for educational purposes only.

  • Students will be familiar with copyright issues relating to online learning.

  • Students will never disclose or publicise personal information.

  • Students will be aware that any usage, including distributing or receiving information, school related or personal, may be monitored for unusual activity, security and/ or network management reasons.

 

Email:

  • Students will only use email with permission from their teacher.

  • Students will not send or receive any material that is illegal, obscene, defamatory or that is intended to annoy or intimidate another person.

  • Students will not reveal their own or other person’s personal details such as address or mobile number or pictures.

  • Students will never arrange a face to face meeting with someone they only know through emails or the internet.

  • Students will note that sending and receiving email attachments is subject to permission from their teacher.

 

Internet Chat:

  • All access to chat rooms is strictly forbidden.

  • Discussion forums and other electronic communication forums will only be used for educational purposes and will always be supervised.

  • Usernames will be used to avoid disclosure of identity.

  • Face to face meetings with someone organised via internet chat will be forbidden.

 

School Website/ Social Media.

The school maintains its own website and uses a number of social media platforms such as facebook and twitter.

  • Students may be given the opportunity to publish projects or school work on www.mountsioncbssecondary.ie in accordance with this policy.

  • Photographs taken at school based events may be published on the school website or on social media platforms but first and last names will never be published without the consent of parents/guardians.

  • The school will aim to focus on group activities when using digital photographs, audio or video clips.

 

Student Personal Devices

This section refers to devices such as mobile phones, ipods, ipads, chromebooks etc.

 

  • Personal devices (Bring your own devices BYODs) may be used in class for educational purposes only but with the express permission of the teacher of that class.They can only be taken out when the teacher requests students to do so and they must be turned off and put away when the teacher informs students that they are no longer required.

  • Devices may not be used for personal communications during class time.

  • Responsibility to keep the device secure rests with the individual owner. The school is not liable for any device stolen or damaged on campus.

  • The unauthorised capture of images, video or audio is in direct breach of the school’s AUP.

  • Connecting or attempting to connect to the school’s network system (wired or wireless) without authorisation is a breach of the school’s AUP.

Sanctions:

Misuse of the internet may result in disciplinary action, including written warnings, withdrawal of access privileges and, in extreme cases, suspension or expulsion . The school also reserves the right to report any illegal activities to the appropriate authorities.
