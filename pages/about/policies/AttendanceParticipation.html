Attendance and Participation Policy

Rationale: The policy is necessary to respond to the:

  • Legislative requirements of the Education Welfare Act 2002 which determines that the school must have an attendance policy

  • Role of the National Education Welfare Board

  • Requirements of the Department of Education and Skills

  • Educational interests of our students

  • Strategy set out in the school’s DEIS plan

Objectives: Research shows that irregular school attendance alters student performance within school, reduces their chances of success and inhibits social development. The policy promotes:

  • Good attendance record and participation in school

  • Develops a good work ethic within the student body

  • Better continuity of participation in class work

  • Increased student confidence and self-esteem arising from regular attendance

  • Improved examination results leading to varied career opportunities

Roles and Responsibilities

Students: It is the responsibility of students to

  • Attend school every day unless for a valid reason

  • Be punctual for all classes

  • Furnish notes in School Journal for expected absence

  • Furnish notes in School Journal for previous un explained absence

  • Get details of work missed due to absence

Subject Teachers

  • Take a roll call for each class

  • Monitor non-attendance and inform the school office

  • Make a list of students attending extracurricular activities for office and staff notice board

Class Tutors

  • Ensure completion of weekly attendance sheet

  • Leave copy of completed weekly attendance sheet in the office each Friday

  • Liaise with parents with regard to non-attendance patterns

  • Liaise with Home School Community Liaison teacher on attendance issues

School/Principal: The Principal will ensure that the school will:

  • Be open for 167 sessions each school year

  • Maintain attendance rolls

  • Accurately record and monitor all absenteeism and lateness

  • Clearly distinguish between absence which is authorised and absence which is unauthorised

  • Submit, termly, absence returns to the National Educational Welfare Board

  • Liaise with the National Educational Welfare Officer regarding absence issues and meet with relevant families

  • Inform the National Educational Welfare Board of students who are suspended from school for six days or more

  • Inform the National Educational Welfare Board when a student has been absent for a period of 20 days or greater and inform them of the reasons for the student’s absence

Board of Management: The Board shall ensure that

  • The statutory obligations of the school with regard to school attendance and participation are adhered to

  • A report on attendance levels in the school is submitted to the Education Welfare Officer

  • That all information is collected and issued in accordance with the Data Protection Act(1988)

  • An evaluation of the process, strategy and policy is carried out regularly

Parents: Parents can do a great deal to support the regular and punctual attendance of their son. Parents should:

  • Ensure that their son attends school regularly, on time, dressed in the school uniform and in a fit condition to learn

  • Ensure that their son stays at school once they have registered

  • Take an active interest in their son’s school life and work

  • Attend parents’ evenings and other school events

  • Ensure that their son completes his/her homework and goes to bed at an appropriate time

  • Be aware of letters from school which their son brings home

  • Ensure that their son only misses school for reasons which are unavoidable or justified, such as illness. Always notify the school as soon as possible – preferably on the first morning – of any absence

  • Confirm this in writing when the son returns to school

  • Avoid booking family holidays during the school term

  • Talk to the school if they are concerned that their son may be reluctant to attend

  • Avoid collecting their son unexpectedly from school as this causes significant disruption to teaching and learning

Student support to alleviate attendance problems:

  • School Completion Programme: The SCP holds a database of target students and tracks their pattern of attendance. This indicates any necessary interventions

  • Student Support Care Team: Weekly meeting analyses non-attendance patterns and decides on appropriate interventions

  • Early identification of students ‘at risk’

  • Treoir: The mentoring group keeps a close monitor of absences for the particular group of students involved in the programme

  • HSCL: The coordinator visits families to discuss reasons for ongoing absences and establishes close links with parents of ‘at risk’ students

  • HSCL visits parents to discuss benefits of regular attendance

  • Meetings with parents and Class Tutor/Principal of chronic absentees

  • Good attendance encouraged at assemblies

  • Certificates for good attendance awarded through JCSP

  • Yearly Mayoral visit promotes good attendance

  • Foroige targeting specific students

  • For chronic attendance issues the Principal and/or SCP liaises with Education Welfare Officer in Tusla

  • Weekly review of attendance figures

  • Monthly review of strategy to deal with chronic cases

Attendance Tracking

  • The school roll is taken every day by the Principal or the school secretary

  • Unless prior notification has been received by the office, parents are contacted by phone to enquire as to reason for student’s absence.

  • A notice, listing all absentee students, is placed on the staff notice board for the attention of the designated Class Tutors

  • The Class Tutor will pursue notes to explain absences

  • Parents are encouraged to use the school Journal to communicate with teachers

Success Criteria

  • Improvement in attendance figures by 3% by 2016/2017

  • Agreed procedures are consistently implemented

  • Chronic case numbers decrease by 5%
