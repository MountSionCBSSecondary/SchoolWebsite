Homework is given by teachers to reinforce the learning that has taken place in class.

The purpose of this document is to:

  • Clarify the responsibilities of students, staff and parents in relation to homework.
  • Encourage the students to put their best effort into homework.
  • Encourage you, the parents, to take an active interest in monitoring homework.
  • Encourage you to communicate with school staff in relation to homework when necessary.

Types of Homework

  • Written work
  • Preparing for tests
  • Reading
  • Research
  • Rote learning

Responsibilities of the Student

  • Write your homework into your Journal.
  • Ask the teacher for help if you don’t understand the homework.
  • Do your homework to the best of your ability.

Responsibilities of the Teacher and School Management

  • Explain the homework.
  • Check that students understand the homework.
  • Give adequate time for students to write the homework in Journal.
  • Teachers check homework is written in Journals
  • Teachers give feedback on homework, taking into account each student’s ability.
  • Teachers acknowledge good effort in homework.
  • Study Skills are taught to the students.
  • The Principal and Deputy Principal carry out occasional checks on students’ Journals.
  • Sanctions may be imposed for repeated poor effort at homework.

Responsibilities of the Parent

  • Write a note in your son’s Journal if he is struggling with, or cannot complete, homework.
  • If your child is in First Year, please read and sign his Journal daily. For other students, please read and sign your son’s Journal weekly.
  • If you have any concerns regarding your son’s ability to do homework on an ongoing basis, please contact the Home/School/Community Liaison teacher.

How much time should homework take?

1st and 2nd year: 1 to 1½ hours each night.

3rd year: 1½ to 2 hours each night.

Senior students: approximately 3 hours, including study time.
